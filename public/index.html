The Moon is lemon-shaped

Despite its appearance in the night sky, our natural satellite is nowhere near round. In fact, the Moon is shaped like a lemon, with flattened poles and bulges on both the near and far side around its equator. This strange shape is thought to have been created during interactions with Earth soon after its formation.

Milky Way smells of rum, raspberries and booze

Astronomers have discovered a gas cloud in the center of our galaxy that contains ethyl formate, the chemical responsible for giving raspberries their flavor and rum its smell.

Venus has longer days than years

Venus has a slow axis rotation which takes about 243 Earth days to complete one full cycle. Interestingly, it takes Venus 225 Earth days to complete one orbit around the Sun, making a day on Venus longer than a year on Venus.

Black holes aren't actually holes

Black holes are incredibly dense objects with gravity so strong that not even light can escape. They are not empty but rather filled with a massive amount of matter packed into a very small area.
